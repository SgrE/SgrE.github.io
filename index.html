About me

I'm currently a postdoc researcher in the Laboratory for Information and Inference Systems (LIONS), École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL), Switzerland, working with Prof. Volkan Cevher. Before that, I spent two years as a postdoc researcher in Department of Electrical Engineering (ESAT-STADIUS), KU Leuven, Belgium, hosted by Prof. Johan A.K. Suykens.

I obtained the Ph.D degree from Institute of Image Processing Pattern Recognition in Department of Automation, Shanghai Jiao Tong University (SJTU) in June 2019, supervised by Prof. Jie Yang, and the B.E. degree in Automation, School of Astronautics, Harbin Institute of Technology (HIT) in 2014.

Research Interests

I’m generally interested in statistical machine learning, centered around kernel methods and learning theory. My main attention focuses on theoretically understanding generalization properties of machine learning based algorithms, especially on over-parameterized models (motivated by neural networks).

  • kernel methods in theory [JMLR20, JMLR21] and indefinite kernels [AISTATS21, ML21 ].

  • kernel approximation via random features and quadrature [TPAMI21]: refer to a survey [TPAMI21]!

  • over-parameterized models in generalization guarantees [AISTATS21,NeurPS22].

Currently I'm also interested in reinforcement learning theory [NeurIPS22], especially from the perspective of function approximation.

Besides, I also take time on some student projects in theoretical-oriented application topics, e.g., robustness [NeurIPS22], NAS [NeurIPS22], OOD [NeurIPS22], and verification [NeurIPS22].
